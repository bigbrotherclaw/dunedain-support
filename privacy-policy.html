Dúnedain Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 5, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Dúnedain collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Dúnedain mobile application and related services. Dúnedain is a fitness, training, and recovery application. By using the app, you acknowledge the practices described in this policy.

1. Scope

This policy applies to information collected through the Dúnedain app, including account information, training data, and data imported from connected third-party services such as Whoop and Garmin. It does not apply to the privacy practices of third-party services themselves.

2. Information We Collect

Depending on how you use the app, we may collect the following categories of information:

  • Account information, such as your email address, authentication provider, display name, and profile image if available.
  • User-provided training information, such as one rep maxes, start dates, workout history, settings, preferences, notes, schedule adjustments, and shared plan data if you choose to use those features.
  • Connected device and fitness data from services you authorize, such as recovery scores, heart rate variability, resting heart rate, sleep metrics, strain, calories, workouts, activities, splits, pace, distance, cadence, elevation, swimming metrics, and related training data.
  • Technical and diagnostic information, such as app usage metadata, timestamps, sync status, and basic logs needed to operate, debug, secure, and improve the service.
  • Support communications, such as the name, email address, and message content you submit through the support form.

3. Connected Account Credentials and Tokens

When you connect third-party services like Whoop or Garmin, we store the access credentials, refresh credentials, tokens, or session material required to maintain those integrations and sync your data. We use these credentials only to provide the features you request, such as importing activity, recovery, sleep, or workout data into the app.

4. How We Use Information

We use information to:

  • create and manage your account;
  • authenticate you and keep you signed in;
  • display your workouts, training schedule, recovery metrics, and progress;
  • sync data from services you choose to connect;
  • generate charts, insights, trends, recommendations, and recovery-aware suggestions;
  • store your preferences and personalize the app experience;
  • provide customer support and respond to questions, bugs, and feature requests;
  • monitor reliability, prevent abuse, protect security, and troubleshoot issues;
  • comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms or protect rights and safety where permitted by law.

5. Legal Bases for Processing

If you are in a jurisdiction that requires a legal basis for processing, we generally rely on one or more of the following:

  • Performance of a contract, when we process information needed to provide the app and requested features.
  • Consent, when you choose to connect Whoop, Garmin, or other third-party services, or where consent is otherwise required.
  • Legitimate interests, such as securing the service, preventing misuse, improving reliability, and responding to support requests, where those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal obligations, where processing is necessary to comply with applicable law.

6. How We Share Information

We do not sell your personal information and we do not share your personal information with data brokers.

We may share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers and infrastructure vendors that help us operate the app, such as authentication, database, hosting, analytics, file storage, and email or support tooling.
  • Connected platforms you choose to use, such as Whoop or Garmin, as part of the functionality you explicitly authorize.
  • Legal or safety reasons, if reasonably necessary to comply with law, respond to lawful requests, protect users, investigate fraud, or enforce our rights.
  • Business transfers, if the app or its assets are involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale, subject to applicable law.

7. Third-Party Services

The app may rely on or integrate with third-party services, including:

  • Supabase, for authentication, database, backend functions, and storage. Privacy Policy: https://supabase.com/privacy
  • Whoop, for connected recovery, sleep, strain, and workout data. Privacy Policy: https://www.whoop.com/us/en/full-privacy-policy/
  • Garmin, for connected activity and health-related data. Privacy Policy: https://www.garmin.com/en-US/privacy/global/
  • Google, if you choose Google sign-in. Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Your use of those services is also governed by their own terms and privacy policies. We are not responsible for the privacy or security practices of third parties.

8. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the service, maintain your account, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Specific retention periods may vary by data type.

  • Account and profile data are generally retained while your account remains active.
  • Connected service tokens and sync data are retained until you disconnect the service, delete your account, or they are otherwise removed in the ordinary operation of the service.
  • Support requests may be retained for operational and recordkeeping purposes.

9. Your Choices and Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to:

  • access the personal information we hold about you;
  • request correction of inaccurate information;
  • request deletion of your information;
  • request a copy of certain information in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
  • object to or restrict certain processing in some circumstances;
  • disconnect third-party integrations and revoke access.

To exercise these rights, contact us using the contact details below. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling certain requests.

10. Account Deletion and Disconnecting Integrations

You may request deletion of your account and associated data by contacting us. You may also disconnect connected services like Whoop or Garmin inside the app or through support, which will stop future syncing and may remove stored connection credentials and related synced data, subject to backup, security, fraud-prevention, and legal retention needs.

11. Data Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards intended to protect personal information. These may include authentication controls, row-level access controls, encrypted transport, restricted backend access, and secure infrastructure providers. However, no system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

12. International Data Transfers

Your information may be processed and stored in countries other than your own, including the United States and other jurisdictions where our service providers operate. By using the app, you understand that your information may be transferred to and processed in those jurisdictions, subject to applicable safeguards where required by law.

13. Children’s Privacy

Dúnedain is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, contact us and we will take appropriate steps to investigate and delete the information where required. If a higher age threshold applies in your jurisdiction for consent to data processing, that threshold also applies.

14. Health and Fitness Information

The app may process information related to your fitness, wellness, training, sleep, activity, and recovery. This information may be considered sensitive in some jurisdictions. We process it only to provide the features you request and improve your experience in the app.

Dúnedain is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified professional before making health or training decisions based on app data.

15. California and Other U.S. State Privacy Disclosures

If you are a California resident or reside in another U.S. state with applicable privacy laws, you may have additional rights, including rights to know, access, correct, delete, and in some cases opt out of certain processing. We do not sell personal information and do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as those terms are defined under certain U.S. privacy laws.

16. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date above. If changes are material, we may provide additional notice within the app or by other appropriate means.
