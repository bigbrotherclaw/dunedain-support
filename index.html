Getting Started

  1. Sign in with your Google account. Your profile and settings are created automatically.
  2. Set your 1 rep maxes (1RMs) in Settings. The app calculates all your working weights from these numbers.
  3. Set your start date — this tells the app where you are in the 6-week loading cycle.
  4. Connect devices (optional) — link your Garmin and/or Whoop accounts for automatic activity tracking.
Tip: If you're new to Tactical Barbell, check out Settings → About This Program for background on the methodology.

Dashboard

The home screen shows:

  • Your Week — a strip showing all 7 days, what's planned, and what you've completed.
  • Today's Workout — the programmed session for today with full details (exercises, sets, weights).
  • Recovery & Strain — if you have Whoop connected, you'll see donut gauges for Sleep, Recovery, and Strain.
  • Upcoming — a collapsible list of the next few days' workouts.

6-Week Loading Cycle

Weights progress automatically based on your 1RMs:

WeekSets × RepsIntensity

After Week 6, the cycle repeats. Re-test or adjust your 1RMs in Settings if you've gotten stronger.

Deadlift

Deadlift follows TB Option 1 — 1 work set per session at the same percentage as your other lifts. This keeps total deadlift volume manageable alongside squats.

Accessories

Each strength day includes an accessory group that rotates:

  • Group A — Chest + Abs
  • Group B — Back + Arms + Abs
  • Group C — Shoulders + Chest + Abs

Conditioning

Conditioning follows the Black Protocol from Tactical Barbell II:

  • 2 HIC sessions/week HIC — High Intensity Conditioning (20–30 min, high effort)
  • 1 Endurance session/week E — Long Steady State or similar (30–60+ min, conversational pace)

HIC Workouts

The app includes all 40 HIC protocols from TB2, organized into categories:

  • #1–24 — Aerobic/Anaerobic (intervals, tempo work, threshold training)
  • #25–36 — General Conditioning (circuits, complexes, carries)
  • #37–40 — Power Development (explosive work, plyometrics)

The app rotates through these automatically, or you can pick your own.

Endurance Sessions

7 endurance session types rotate weekly:

  • LSS — Long Steady State (run, bike, swim, ruck — keep heart rate in Zone 2)
  • Ruck Up — Weighted rucking
  • Triples — 3 activities back-to-back (e.g., run, bike, swim)
  • Fun Run variants — Fartlek-style or easy trail running
  • Warrior Run — Obstacle/terrain-focused run

Easy Weeks

Every 3rd week is an Easy Week — conditioning is dialed back to let your body recover. The app handles this automatically.

Logging Workouts

There are three ways to log a workout:

  1. Auto-detection — If you have Garmin or Whoop connected, the app detects completed activities and prompts you to log them with one tap. It pulls in duration, heart rate, distance, pace, and strain automatically.
  2. Manual log — Tap today's workout card, complete the exercises, and log it with your actual weights and reps.
  3. Quick log — Mark a workout as done without detailed tracking.
Tip: For multi-activity sessions (like a run + swim for LSS), the app will detect and include all activities, not just the first one.

Garmin Integration

Connect your Garmin account in Settings to automatically pull:

  • Activity type, duration, and distance
  • Heart rate (average and max)
  • Pace and splits
  • Cadence, stride length, elevation gain
  • Swimming metrics (stroke count, SWOLF, pool length)
  • VO2 Max and training effect

Garmin data syncs when you open the app and periodically while it's open.

Note: Garmin uses a two-step OAuth process. If your connection drops, reconnect in Settings → Garmin.

Whoop Integration

Connect your Whoop account in Settings to pull:

  • Recovery — recovery score, HRV (RMSSD), resting heart rate
  • Sleep — sleep performance percentage, duration, stages
  • Strain — daily strain score, calories burned
  • Workouts — activity strain, heart rate, and heart rate zones

Whoop data appears on the dashboard as three donut gauges (Sleep, Recovery, Strain) and is merged with Garmin data for workout logging.

How Merging Works

When both devices are connected, the app uses Garmin for mechanics (pace, distance, splits) and Whoop for strain (strain score, calories, HR zones). Activities are matched by time overlap.

Sync Schedule

  • On app launch — syncs the last 7 days
  • Every 15 minutes — syncs the last 3 days while the app is open
  • First connection — backfills 30 days of data
  • Webhooks — Whoop pushes real-time updates for recovery, sleep, and workouts

Calendar & Swapping Days

The calendar view shows your full training schedule with completed workouts marked. If you need to rearrange your week:

  1. Open the Calendar tab
  2. Drag and drop any workout day to swap it with another
  3. Swaps are saved per-week, so your base template stays intact

This is useful when life gets in the way — move your strength day to accommodate travel, recovery, or scheduling conflicts.

Stats & Progress
